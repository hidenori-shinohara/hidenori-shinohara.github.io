# Proposition
Find the derivative of the function $T(z) = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}$ where $a, b, c, d \in \mathbb{C}$ with $ad - bc \ne 0$.
When is $T'(z) = 0$?

# Solution

$$
\begin{align*}
  T'(z)
    &= \frac{(cz + d)a - (az + b)c}{(cz + d)^2} \\
    &= \frac{ad - bc}{(cz + d)^2}.
\end{align*}
$$

Therefore, $\forall z \in \mathbb{C}$, $T'(z) \ne 0$.
