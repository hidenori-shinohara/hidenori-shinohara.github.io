# Proposition
Give an example that shows that the product of two harmonic functions is not necessarily harmonic.

# Solution
Let $u(x, y) = x$.
Then $$u_{xx} = u_{yy} = 0$$.
Let $v = u \cdot u$.
Then $$v_{xx} = 2, v_{yy} = 0$$.
Thus $v$ is not harmonic.
